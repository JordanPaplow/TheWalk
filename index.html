The Walk
September 24, 2022
It's is a cool and sunny Saturday morning.

In a futile attempt at slowing my innevitable devolution into some freakish work-from-home troll. I venture out into the city streets.

I exit the Subaru under watchful gaze of St Augustine

I parked here, in font of the church next to my office, no good reason, just force of habit.

It's roughly 3 miles to downtown from this part of Lawrenceville.

As good a destination as any.

I walk down Butler street, the main drag of Lawrenceville.

A handfull of people wander about.
Getting coffee, walking their dogs... the normal Saturday commotion.
The morning light
paints subtle compostions
on the brick walls of the city.

Pittsburgh is a labirynth
of textures

A citidel of brick
puncturing the forest.

Crumbling red
in a rolling sea of green.
So unlike the sprawling beige
of my Californian childhood.

Breathing in the air
Cool and crisp.

What a strange thing,
muscles expanding and contracting.

An action essential, but so often ignored.
